What is Climate Change?

Climate change refers to long-term shifts in temperatures and weather patterns. These shifts may be natural, such as through variations in the solar cycle. But since the 1800s, human activities have been the main driver of climate change, primarily due to burning fossil fuels like coal, oil and gas.

Increase in global sea level

The rate of sea level rise has been accelerating over the years. Global mean sea level has risen about 8–9 inches (21–24 centimeters) since 1880, with about a third of that coming in just the last two and a half decades.

C02 emissions

CO2 emissions are one of the major causes of climate change. The increase in CO2 emissions over the years has led to many negative effects like temperature increase, rise in sea level etc. as illustrated before. It is important to understand how the different countries have been contributing to this issue. Conclusion

Clearly, we see that many countries have achieved their development by expending enough CO2 to even put us into this situation of global warming. The fact that many other countries have achieved the same development through leaner means shows that it is definitely possible.

Now, the question stands that should the developing countries be held to standards which were clearly broken by these developed countries during their own development or should the developing countries be allowed to develop unrestrained and in the process push the world over the edge when it comes to climate change.

These are answers the world's leaders are trying to resolve but the records show what the records show with respect to past CO2 consumption. Ideally, the developing countries can realize that renewable energy and sustainable development are what the future needs. It will, however, be a difficult journey for them as they battle new-founded laws and regulations enforced by countries who have reached their current status unhindered by any such rules.
